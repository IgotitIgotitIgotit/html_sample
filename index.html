Creation Background

"Dragon Ball" (Japanese: ドラゴンボール, English: DRAGON BALL) is the first animation TV series of the "Dragon Ball Series" produced by Toei Animation, adapted from the work of the famous manga artist Akira Toriyama (episodes 1-194) [1]. It was broadcast on Fuji TV from February 26, 1986, to April 19, 1989, with a total of 153 episodes.

Plot Summary

A long, long time ago, there was an ancient legend that there were seven round balls called Dragon Balls scattered around the world. Whoever gathered all seven Dragon Balls and recited the incantation could summon the dragon, which would grant any wish.

Son Goku, who lives deep in the mountains, is highly skilled and, by chance, leaves the mountains with the genius scientist Bulma to search for the seven Dragon Balls scattered around the world. During this journey, he meets the perverted Master Roshi and Oolong, Yamcha, who blushes whenever he sees a woman, and the arrogant monk Krillin. They not only experience various adventures and strange encounters but also get involved in a series of hilarious stories. Of course, there are also many evil individuals who seek the Dragon Balls for selfish desires, leading to numerous fierce battles with Goku and his friends. To fulfill their wishes, surpass themselves, and become stronger, the romantic adventure of Goku and his friends in the big world begins like this!

Story Background More Details

Dragon Ball

There are five types of Dragon Balls in the world of Dragon Ball, with seven in total. Each Dragon Ball has between one and seven red stars. By making a wish to the dragon, any desire can be fulfilled. To find the Dragon Balls, Bulma invented the Dragon Radar during her teenage years, thus beginning the journey to find the Dragon Balls.

Flying Nimbus

A gift given by Master Korin to the young Master Roshi, only those who are pure of heart can ride on it. The original form is a large cloud outside Korin Tower. It is impervious to bullets, shells, and all physical attacks, but can be dispersed by attacks from the Demon Clan. Master Roshi, due to his perverted nature, could not use it, so he gave it to Son Goku.

Power Pole

Appeared as Son Goku's weapon. Red, undecorated, and a cylindrical stick that only a child's hand can grasp. Originally used to connect from the top of Korin Tower to the above God's Palace, but was borrowed by Master Roshi from Korin, and later given to Goku by Grandpa Gohan as a weapon.

Senzu Bean

A magical food cultivated by the legendary Master Korin, who resides atop Korin Tower, provided specifically for the Z warriors when they are hungry.

Capsule

A high-tech product developed by Bulma's family business, a small capsule capable of storing items like motorcycles and cars.

Super Saiyan God

Super Saiyan God is a new concept introduced in the Dragon Ball universe, making its first appearance in the movie "Battle of Gods".
